> Five criteria that make architectural decisions sustainable: strategic, measurable and manageable, achievable and realistic, rooted in requirements, and timeless.

> Source: [Sustainable Architectural Design Decisions](https://www.infoq.com/articles/sustainable-architectural-design-decisions/) (InfoQ).

To define decision sustainability in detail, the authors derived five criteria. The notes below summarize them.

## Strategic

When deciding, consider the strategic consequences — particularly long-term impact such as future operations and maintenance effort. A locally optimal choice that creates an ongoing operational burden is not sustainable.

## Measurable and manageable

You should be able to measure and evaluate a decision's outcome over time against objective criteria, ideally numeric ones (as propagated by quality-attribute scenarios and workshops).

Capturing every fine-grained decision isn't possible, so limit the *granularity* of recorded decisions to a useful level of detail — for example, the creation of a design class, not every method signature. Coarser granularity leads to a more sustainable set of decisions and fewer traceability links, and limiting dependencies between decisions reduces the ripple effect of future changes.

## Achievable and realistic

The rationale for fitting a solution to a problem should be chosen pragmatically and made explicit. Architects should signal that they have avoided over- and under-engineering — explicitly applying the "good enough" approach where it fits.

## Rooted in requirements

Decision making should be grounded in domain-specific architecting experience and context. It should account for the company environment, project requirements and constraints, and the development team's current skills, training budget, and process.

## Timeless

Decisions should rest on experience and knowledge unlikely to be outdated soon. Where possible, prefer platform-neutral architectural patterns and tactics over choices tied to a specific vendor or short-lived technology.
