> Eight practical guidelines for producing sustainable architectural decisions: start lean, prioritize what matters, expand only what's important, reuse existing knowledge, and keep traceability in sync.

> Source: [Sustainable Architectural Design Decisions](https://www.infoq.com/articles/sustainable-architectural-design-decisions/) (InfoQ).

The authors distilled the following guidelines from their work. They serve both as guidance during decision-making and as a checklist for assessing whether existing decisions are sustainable.

1. **Start lean.** Use a minimalistic approach for the initial decision documentation. Heavy templates discourage writing things down.

2. **Prioritize.** Capture every decision that is important enough to document and necessary for understanding the target architecture. Don't try to capture everything.

3. **Expand only what's important.** Only after the initial work is done — and decision-makers are confident the decision won't need to be revised soon — invest in a full-blown template for the particularly important ones.

4. **Use the lean version as the overview.** The minimalistic version from step 1 remains useful as a short overview of the detailed decisions, and as the entire record for trivial or obvious decisions.

5. **Reuse existing architectural knowledge.** Wherever possible, draw on guidance models or other established sources. Review, extend, and tailor that knowledge to fit your specific context.

6. **Establish traceability.** Ensure traceability links exist between decisions and both their driving requirements and the architectural designs or code that implement them.

7. **Keep traceability in sync.** Provide automated consistency checking so traceability links stay current after a change. Limit the number of dependencies between decisions and other software artifacts to keep that automation tractable.

8. **Apply the guidelines rigorously to the justifications.** Justifications — the rationale — are the most important part of a decision record. They are what makes the decision *useful* to future readers. Treat them as the load-bearing element of the documentation, not as a closing paragraph.
