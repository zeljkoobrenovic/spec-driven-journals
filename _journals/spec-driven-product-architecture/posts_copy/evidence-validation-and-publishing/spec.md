# Spec: Evidence, validation, and publishing

## Intent

Close the first article series by explaining how evidence discipline, validators, and generated static documentation make AI-authored product architecture reviewable and maintainable.

## Audience

Product architects, AI-agent operators, engineering leaders, and repository maintainers.

## Success criteria

- [ ] Reader understands why evidence and assumptions must be separated.
- [ ] Reader understands grounding as the explicit connection between dreamed product architecture and explored reality.
- [ ] Reader knows what deterministic validation can and cannot prove.
- [ ] Reader sees generated static documentation as review output, not source of truth.
- [ ] Reader can start a first modeling session with a practical sequence.

## Non-goals

- Full validator documentation.
- Instructions for running every generator group.
- External evidence-source taxonomy.

## Open questions

- Whether this article should become two articles later: one on evidence and validation, one practical getting-started guide.
