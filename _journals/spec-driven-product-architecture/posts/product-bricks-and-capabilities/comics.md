How outcomes become buildable — capabilities, bricks, and the honest arrows between them, in eight panels.

![Comic panel: a boxy robot holds a card labeled TRIPS, torn between a poster of happy riders and a wall of servers.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-01-outcome-or-inventory.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *Every product model faces the same question: is this what the product does, or how it does it?*

![Comic panel: an architect stands at a broken bridge between a cloud of vision prose and a pile of machinery.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-02-the-broken-bridge.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *Stay at the outcome level and you get strategy prose; jump to systems and you get a technical inventory.*

![Comic panel: a robot prints identical generic brick sets for three different domain boards while the architect frowns.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-03-generic-platform-filler.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *The documented drift patterns: feature lists, technology catalogs, and the same generic platform filler for every domain.*

![Comic panel: an architect draws an outcome card connected to a row of labeled bricks on a whiteboard.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-04-capabilities-meet-bricks.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *The decision: capabilities keep the language of outcomes; bricks name the buildable, ownable units beneath them.*

![Comic panel: a brick opened like a toolbox showing layered compartments for UI, API, bus, service, and integration modules.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-05-inside-a-brick.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *Modules in layers — UI, interfaces, bus, services, integrations — give a brick enough shape, not a full solution design.*

![Comic panel: an architect traces dependency arrows between bricks while a robot points at a missing brick drawn as a dotted outline.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-06-honest-arrows.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Dependencies are where the model gets honest: a missing or unowned brick means the capability is under-specified.*

![Comic panel: an architect and a robot tend a model board, re-inking faded trace lines between outcome cards and bricks.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-07-the-upkeep.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *The cost: keeping outcome language and implementation language connected is continuous work, not a one-time drawing.*

![Comic panel: an architect and a robot click a capability card and a brick together like puzzle pieces.](assets/images/product-bricks-and-capabilities/comic-08-need-each-other.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *Capabilities without bricks are aspirations; bricks without capabilities are inventory. The useful model has both.*
