Why delivery, teams, and roadmaps belong inside the architecture — in eight panels.

![Comic panel: three closed doors labeled architecture, delivery, and teams while an architect watches a robot carry memos between them.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-01-three-separate-rooms.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *Architecture is called structure, delivery is called process, teams are called organization — but they describe one system.*

![Comic panel: a robot pushes a cart of release boxes toward unlabeled gray shapes, holding a plan with only dates.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-02-change-without-a-what.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *A delivery plan that ignores architecture cannot explain what is being changed — and vice versa.*

![Comic panel: a cracked brick with an unreadable owner tag under a flickering light as the architect shines a flashlight on it.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-03-hidden-ownership.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *The anti-pattern: hidden ownership — a brick nobody owns is changed by everyone and maintained by no one.*

![Comic panel: an architect pins release cards, team pennants, and roadmap arrows onto one connected board of customers and bricks.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-04-one-connected-model.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *The decision: delivery, ownership, objectives, releases, and roadmap overlays live in the same domain model as customers and bricks.*

![Comic panel: a team plants a flag on a labeled brick with ropes connecting it to neighboring bricks owned by other teams.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-05-teams-own-bricks.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *Teams own bricks, not just tasks — and the dependency view exposes the coordination work between them.*

![Comic panel: a robot lays a transparent roadmap sheet over the architecture board so every arrow touches a brick or team.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-06-roadmap-overlay.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *A strong roadmap item points back to customers, KPIs, capabilities, bricks, and teams — an overlay, not a separate plan.*

![Comic panel: an architect traces one glowing thread through stations for customer, KPI, capability, brick, team, release, and evidence.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-07-the-trace.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *The payoff is the trace: from riders under time pressure to dispatch bricks, the matching team, the release, and the evidence.*

![Comic panel: a team debates around the connected model board while the robot holds a sign reading it answers back.](assets/images/delivery-teams-and-roadmaps/comic-08-the-operating-test.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *The operating test: a model is real architecture when it helps decide what to prioritize, who owns it, and what's at risk.*
