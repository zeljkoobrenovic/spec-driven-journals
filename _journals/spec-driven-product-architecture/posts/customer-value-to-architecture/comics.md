How customer value becomes architecture without getting lost on the way — in eight panels.

![Comic panel: a robot presents a systems diagram while an architect points at an empty chair labeled customer.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-01-starting-too-late.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *The first architectural question is not 'which systems?' — it is 'whose progress are we supporting?'*

![Comic panel: a machine flattens a colorful customer portrait into a pile of identical feature tickets.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-02-flattened-into-tickets.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *By the time teams discuss systems, the customer model is often flattened into feature requests.*

![Comic panel: a robot holds a blank cutout labeled the user, hiding five very different customers behind it.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-03-one-generic-user.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *Anti-pattern: one generic persona — every downstream artifact ends up sounding the same.*

![Comic panel: an architect redirects a robot from a sign reading use search to an arrow reading get there on time.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-04-jobs-as-progress.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *A job describes the progress a customer is making — not the feature they click.*

![Comic panel: four customers pull ropes that stretch a building into shape while an architect sketches it.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-05-different-pull-different-shape.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *The decision: customer groups come first — their different needs are how the architecture finds its shape.*

![Comic panel: an architect and a robot descend nine labeled steps connected by one glowing thread.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-06-the-value-chain.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Customer value stays visible down the chain: group, job, outcome, KPI, horizon, capability, brick, release, team.*

![Comic panel: an architect swaps a fuzzy chain link labeled experience for a crisp gauge-shaped link.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-07-the-upkeep.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *The cost: the chain is a maintained artifact — vague KPIs and slogan horizons quietly rot it.*

![Comic panel: disconnected gray bricks on a shelf versus the same bricks assembled and connected to a customer.](assets/images/customer-value-to-architecture/comic-08-catalog-or-architecture.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *Without the chain, bricks are a technical catalog. With it, they are a product architecture.*
