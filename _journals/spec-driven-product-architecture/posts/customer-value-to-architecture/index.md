> Spec-Driven Product Architecture starts with customer progress, then carries that context through KPIs, strategy horizons, capabilities, product bricks, delivery, and team ownership.

Architecture work often starts too late.

By the time teams discuss systems, services, APIs, and integrations, the customer model may already be flattened into feature requests. Product architecture then becomes a translation exercise: engineers infer the real product shape from tickets, roadmaps, and stakeholder memory.

Spec-Driven Product Architecture changes the order. It starts with customer value and keeps that value connected to architecture decisions.

## Start With Customer Groups

The first architectural question is not "which systems do we need?" It is "whose progress are we supporting?"

A strong product domain separates customer groups that have meaningfully different needs, constraints, risks, or success criteria. In a ride-sharing marketplace, riders, drivers, fleet operators, business travel administrators, and marketplace operators do not experience the product in the same way. In an internal developer platform, application developers, platform engineers, security reviewers, reliability engineers, and engineering leaders may all be customers of different parts of the domain.

That separation matters for architecture because different groups pull the system in different directions.

| Customer distinction | Architecture consequence |
| --- | --- |
| The customer needs real-time confidence. | The domain needs reliable state, live signals, clear events, and strong observability. |
| The customer makes a procurement or compliance decision. | The domain needs evidence, approvals, access controls, auditability, and trust documentation. |
| The customer operates at high frequency. | The domain needs ergonomic workflows, low latency, and automation. |
| The customer resolves exceptions. | The domain needs case management, support tooling, escalation paths, and recoverability. |

Customer grouping is not an empathy exercise bolted onto architecture. It is one of the first ways the architecture discovers its shape.

## Model Jobs As Progress

Jobs to be done should describe the progress a customer is trying to make, not the product feature they click.

Weak job: "Use search."

Stronger job: "Find a stay that feels trustworthy for a family trip within budget and schedule constraints."

Weak job: "Open a deployment dashboard."

Stronger job: "Understand whether a production change is safe enough to release and recover quickly if it fails."

This distinction matters for AI agents. If the agent models jobs as feature usage, it will produce a feature inventory. If it models jobs as progress, it can reason about outcomes, frictions, capabilities, and missing architecture.

The project guidance is especially strict about two journey stages:

- **Discovery** means how customers become aware of the offer before active use.
- **Evaluation** means how customers decide whether the product is right before trial or commitment.

Those stages should not be reused as labels for in-product search or task execution. Keeping the language clean prevents the model from mixing market acquisition, product usage, and operational workflows.

## Use KPIs To Make Value Operational

Customer value becomes operational when it has measurable signals.

Spec-Driven Product Architecture uses KPI pyramids and north-star metrics to connect high-level value to measurable outcomes. The goal is not to create a vanity dashboard. The goal is to make the product architecture testable.

For example:

| Domain concern | Possible KPI direction |
| --- | --- |
| Riders need reliable trips. | Request-to-match time, pickup success rate, ETA accuracy, cancellation rate. |
| Developers need usable golden paths. | Time to first successful deployment, policy exception rate, paved-road adoption. |
| Enterprise users need governed change. | Decision traceability, application inventory freshness, roadmap dependency visibility. |
| Marketplace sellers need healthy demand. | Listing conversion, dispute rate, fulfillment accuracy, repeat buyer rate. |

KPI leaves should be concrete. A branch named "Experience" is not enough. The model should say what observable signal proves the experience is improving.

## Add Strategy Horizons

A product architecture model should not only describe the current product. It should say where the product is going.

The source project commonly uses 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year horizons. These horizons help AI agents and reviewers distinguish near-term execution from long-term ambition.

Each horizon should answer:

- What is the focus?
- Which product theme dominates?
- Which customer KPI should improve?
- Which business KPI should improve?
- Which milestones show progress?

The horizon is where product vision becomes a planning constraint. A capability that is irrelevant to the 1-year horizon may still matter as a 3-year investment. A product brick that is operationally critical today may need to be contained, replaced, or sunset by a later horizon.

## Carry Customer Value Into Architecture

The model becomes powerful when customer value stays visible as it moves downward:

1. Customer group.
2. Job to be done.
3. Desired outcome.
4. KPI signal.
5. Strategy horizon.
6. Product capability.
7. Product brick.
8. Delivery release or initiative.
9. Owning team.

This chain does not need to be perfect in the first draft. But the chain should exist. It gives future AI sessions something to inspect and improve.

### Running Example: Ride Sharing Marketplace

A ride-sharing marketplace can make the chain concrete:

| Model step | Ride-sharing example |
| --- | --- |
| Customer group | Riders who need reliable on-demand travel under time pressure. |
| Job to be done | Get matched with a trustworthy trip that arrives when expected. |
| Desired outcome | A completed trip with accurate ETA, transparent price, and safe handoff. |
| KPI signal | Request-to-match time, pickup success rate, ETA accuracy, cancellation rate. |
| Strategy horizon | Improve reliability in the current year before expanding advanced marketplace optimization. |
| Product capability | Book and complete a reliable trip. |
| Product brick | Trip request, matching and dispatch, pricing, payments, and support/recovery bricks. |
| Delivery release or initiative | Improve dispatch resilience and cancellation recovery. |
| Owning team | Marketplace matching team, coordinated with pricing, payments, trust, and support teams. |
| Evidence | Product analytics, support tickets, incident data, driver availability signals, and financial impact. |

Without this chain, product bricks become a technical catalog. With the chain, they become a product architecture.

## What To Watch For

The failure modes are predictable:

- Customer groups are too generic, so every downstream artifact sounds the same.
- Jobs describe product features instead of customer progress.
- KPIs use vague labels that cannot be measured.
- Strategy horizons are slogans with no milestones.
- Capabilities and product bricks appear without any connection to customer outcomes.
- Teams own systems, but no one can explain which customer value the systems support.

Those failures are not only editorial. They reduce the usefulness of AI agents. The agent cannot maintain coherence that the model does not express.
