> **Status**: Accepted (2020-01-21)
>
> **Decision**: We will move to a 4-day workweek (Monday–Thursday, 10 hours per day) starting next quarter for all full-time employees, with flexible scheduling for part-time staff. The change will be reviewed after six months.

## Context

We currently run a standard 5-day, 8-hour schedule. Over the past year we have seen falling productivity, rising burnout, and elevated turnover. At the same time, an internal survey showed strong employee preference for a more flexible schedule and better work–life balance, and several studies on companies that adopted a 4-day workweek report comparable or higher output with fewer hours worked.

We want to act on this evidence rather than wait for conditions to worsen, and we want to do so in a way we can measure and reverse if it doesn't work.

## Decision

Starting next quarter, the standard workweek will be four days, Monday through Thursday, at 10 hours per day for full-time employees. Part-time employees may negotiate flexible schedules with their managers. All other compensation and benefits remain unchanged.

## Rationale

1. **Employee feedback.** The internal survey returned a clear majority in favor of a compressed week. Acting on that signal directly addresses the engagement and retention problems we are already seeing.
2. **Evidence from other organizations.** Companies that have piloted a 4-day week consistently report higher productivity, lower absenteeism, and improved well-being — without proportional drops in output.
3. **Hiring and retention.** A 4-day week is a differentiator in our hiring market and a tangible reason for current employees to stay.
4. **Strategic fit.** The change is consistent with our stated goal of being a healthier, higher-performing organization rather than one that competes on hours worked.

## Implications

1. **Communication.** The decision will be announced company-wide, followed by team-level discussions with managers to surface concerns and edge cases.
2. **Policy.** HR will update leave, benefits, and scheduling policies to reflect the new baseline, and clarify how holidays falling on a non-working Friday are handled.
3. **Management practice.** Managers will be coached on running effective four-day weeks: protecting focus time, avoiding compensating overwork, and measuring output rather than hours.
4. **Customer-facing coverage.** Teams whose work requires Friday coverage will design a rotation so the change does not degrade service.
5. **Review.** After six months we will evaluate satisfaction, retention, output, and customer impact. If the results are negative, we will return to a 5-day schedule rather than persist out of inertia.

## Conclusion

We expect a 4-day week to improve employee well-being and retention without harming output, based on internal demand and external evidence. We are committing to the change and to an honest review at the six-month mark.
