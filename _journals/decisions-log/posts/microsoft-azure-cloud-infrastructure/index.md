> **Status**: Accepted (2020-01-21)
>
> **Decision**: We will adopt Microsoft Azure as the cloud infrastructure for the organization, replacing the on-premises footprint over time.

## Context

We are moving from traditional on-premises infrastructure to a public cloud and need to pick a primary provider. We evaluated AWS, GCP, Microsoft Azure, and IBM Cloud across service coverage, scalability, security and compliance, hybrid-cloud support, and pricing. Azure came out best against our requirements, in particular our need to keep some workloads on-premises during the transition.

## Decision

We will adopt Microsoft Azure as the organization's cloud infrastructure. The CIO sponsors the decision; migration and platform ownership sit with the cloud infrastructure team.

## Rationale

1. **Comprehensive service catalog.** Azure covers the IaaS, PaaS, and SaaS surface we need — compute, storage, networking, analytics, and integration services — without forcing us to mix providers.
2. **Scalability and flexibility.** Azure scales elastically and supports a broad range of operating systems, languages, and frameworks, so existing teams keep working with familiar stacks.
3. **Security and compliance.** Azure is certified against the standards we care about (ISO, SOC, HIPAA, PCI DSS) and ships network security groups, firewalls, and DDoS protection out of the box.
4. **Hybrid-cloud support.** Azure's hybrid story makes it straightforward to keep some workloads on-premises during migration and to retain that option long-term where needed.
5. **Pay-as-you-go pricing.** Consumption-based pricing aligns spend with usage and avoids the up-front capital commitments of on-premises hardware.

## Implications

1. **Migration plan.** A phased migration plan from on-premises to Azure will be defined, prioritized, and owned by the cloud infrastructure team.
2. **Training.** Engineering, operations, and security teams need ramp-up on Azure services and tooling.
3. **Cost management.** Pay-as-you-go pricing requires ongoing monitoring and right-sizing to stay within budget.
4. **Vendor concentration.** Standardizing on Azure increases dependency on a single provider; we will keep portability in mind for workloads where preserving it is cheap.
5. **Review.** The decision will be reviewed periodically to confirm Azure continues to match our needs and that the expected benefits are being realized.

## Conclusion

Azure offers the best fit for our requirements, particularly because of its hybrid support and breadth of managed services. The investment in migration and training is expected to pay back through faster delivery, better availability, and more predictable costs.
