> **Status**: Accepted (2020-01-21)
>
> **Decision**: We will adopt Kubernetes as our container orchestration platform for our growing cloud-native application portfolio.

## Context

Our legacy deployment platform is too slow and not agile enough to keep up with the way our cloud-native portfolio is growing. We need an orchestration platform that lets us scale services efficiently while retaining ease of use and supporting frequent change. We evaluated Docker Swarm, Kubernetes, and Apache Mesos against scalability, fault tolerance, community traction, and ecosystem.

## Decision

We will adopt Kubernetes as the container orchestration platform for the present and foreseeable future. New services will be deployed on Kubernetes by default; existing services on the legacy platform will be migrated over time.

## Rationale

1. **Scalability.** Kubernetes's design is built for scaling: it handles workload-level scaling and cluster-level scaling as our needs grow, without forcing us to swap platforms when we cross size thresholds.
2. **Decentralized architecture.** The master/worker topology avoids a single point of failure and supports resilient, self-healing deployments.
3. **Community support.** Kubernetes has the largest and most active open-source community in this space. That translates into more contributors, documentation, hires, and answers when something goes wrong.
4. **Ecosystem.** A wide ecosystem of tools — container registries, CI/CD integrations, service meshes, storage and observability tooling — plugs into Kubernetes natively, reducing the amount of glue we have to build ourselves.

## Implications

1. **Learning curve.** Kubernetes is more complex than the alternatives. Engineering and operations teams will need investment in training and hands-on time before we depend on it for critical workloads.
2. **Operational ownership.** Whether we self-host or use a managed offering, we need clear ownership for cluster lifecycle, upgrades, capacity, and incident response.
3. **Migration plan.** Existing workloads on the legacy platform need a phased migration plan, not a flag day.
4. **Tooling and standards.** We will define standard patterns for manifests, secrets, ingress, observability, and CI/CD integration so that teams converge on a small number of paved roads.

## Conclusion

Kubernetes gives us the scalability, resilience, and ecosystem we need to grow our cloud-native portfolio without re-platforming again later. The operational investment is real, but it is the right floor to build the next few years of delivery on.
