> **Status**: Accepted (2020-01-21)
>
> **Decision**: We will adopt Amazon Web Services (AWS) as our cloud infrastructure provider, on the basis of AWS's service breadth, global footprint, security posture, and pay-as-you-go pricing.

## Context

The business is growing fast and we need cloud infrastructure that is reliable, scalable, and can absorb both current and projected demand. We evaluated the leading cloud providers against our requirements for service coverage, operational maturity, availability, security, and cost flexibility.

## Decision

We will adopt AWS as the primary cloud infrastructure for the organization. Migration of existing workloads and provisioning of new ones will go through the cloud infrastructure team.

## Rationale

1. **Service breadth.** AWS offers the widest catalog of services we need today and a credible roadmap for what we expect to need next, reducing the chance that we hit a capability gap mid-migration.
2. **Operational maturity.** AWS has a long track record of large-scale production deployments and an extensive ecosystem of partners, training, and support resources, which lowers the cost of finding answers and hiring people who already know the platform.
3. **Global footprint and availability.** Multi-region data centers and mature high-availability building blocks make it straightforward to meet our uptime and data-locality needs.
4. **Security and compliance.** AWS's security framework, certifications, and shared-responsibility model match what our risk and compliance functions require.
5. **Pay-as-you-go pricing.** Consumption-based pricing lets us scale up and down with demand and avoid the up-front capital commitments of on-premises infrastructure.

## Implications

1. **Training.** Engineering, operations, and security teams will need ramp-up on AWS services and AWS-specific best practices.
2. **Migration cost.** Moving existing infrastructure carries a one-time cost in engineering time and may incur temporary parallel-run costs.
3. **Ownership.** The cloud infrastructure team owns AWS adoption, accounts, guardrails, and incident response, and is the escalation path for issues.
4. **Vendor concentration.** Standardizing on AWS increases our dependency on a single provider; we will keep portability in mind for workloads where it is cheap to preserve.
5. **Review cadence.** The decision will be reviewed annually to confirm that AWS continues to fit our needs and that we are realizing the expected benefits.

## Conclusion

AWS is the best match for our scale, growth trajectory, and operational requirements. We expect the investment in migration and training to pay back through faster delivery, better availability, and more predictable costs.
