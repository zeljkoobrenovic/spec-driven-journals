Why building on generative models feels like fashion — in eight panels.

![Comic panel: a robot bursts in announcing a new AI model while the product lead pins a garment labeled 'our product'.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-01-new-model-drops.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *Every major model release can change what is possible, what customers expect, and what should be rebuilt.*

![Comic panel: two doors, one where AI is a tool for builders and one where AI is the product material itself.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-02-two-conversations.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *AI-assisted development is a productivity shift; AI-powered product development is an operating-model shift.*

![Comic panel: three increasingly different bolts of model fabric beside a dated garment made from the oldest one.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-03-changing-fabric.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *The model is part of the product's material — and the material changes from outside, in short seasons.*

![Comic panel: a benchmark-winning garment fits the golden-scenario mannequin badly, with a splitting seam.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-04-beautiful-but-wrong.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *'Better model' is not 'better product': general gains can break your golden scenarios, controls, costs, or promises.*

![Comic panel: a six-station loop on the wall — scout, design, fit, evaluate, release, archive — walked by the duo.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-05-seasonal-loop.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *The rhythm is seasonal: scout the material, design a collection, fit on real users, evaluate, release selectively, archive the learning.*

![Comic panel: a garment passes through evaluation archways for job fit, cost, and product promise before shipping.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-06-fitting-room.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Evaluation is the fitting room: demos must still fit the job, justify the cost, and keep the promise customers trust.*

![Comic panel: a mannequin buried under every new fabric and gadget while the product lead holds shears and one clean sketch.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-07-fad-driven.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *The unhealthy version is fad-driven development: novelty without coherence. Good fashion edits.*

![Comic panel: a fixed wardrobe labeled promise, quality bar, and trust surrounded by rotating seasonal racks.](assets/images/fashion-driven-software-development/comic-08-stable-core.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *Fashion changes, but not everything changes: the stable core is what lets the product rebuild fast without losing itself.*
