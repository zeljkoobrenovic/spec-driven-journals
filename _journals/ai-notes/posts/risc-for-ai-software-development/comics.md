Why smaller platform surfaces make AI-generated software easier to trust — in eight panels.

![Comic panel: a robot proud of a towering pile of generated code while the engineer holds a tiny magnifying glass.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-01-trust-bottleneck.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *The bottleneck is not how much code AI can generate — it is how much of it engineers can understand, verify, and trust.*

![Comic panel: a robot overwhelmed by a giant wall of levers labeled with framework and dependency names.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-02-giant-instruction-set.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *Frameworks, libraries, SaaS APIs, plugins, conventions: this is the instruction set the agent — and its reviewer — must decode.*

![Comic panel: a small clean panel with six labeled buttons replaces a huge tangled console under a dust sheet.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-03-the-risc-move.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *The RISC move: prefer a smaller, more regular platform surface when it makes generated systems easier to inspect and maintain.*

![Comic panel: a robot works at a workbench of text files, diffs, and tests while the engineer reviews a diff sheet.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-04-text-first-workbench.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *Files, diffs, tests, and version control: a reduced instruction set where the agent's work remains visible and reviewable.*

![Comic panel: a balance scale weighing a huge chained dependency wheel against a small tested local wheel.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-05-wheel-economics.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *AI changes the economics: is the imported wheel really simpler and safer than the small wheel we can generate, test, and own?*

![Comic panel: the engineer stops the robot from rebuilding crypto, auth, and payments behind a vault door.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-06-hard-wheels-vault.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Judgment still rules: security-sensitive, edge-case-heavy wheels are reused; only small, local, testable wheels get generated.*

![Comic panel: dozens of near-duplicate little wheels clutter the floor while the engineer holds a catalog of blessed primitives.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-07-sprawl-warning.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *The risks of RISC thinking: local-code sprawl, fragmentation, false confidence. Reduction needs stewardship, not ideology.*

![Comic panel: the engineer and the robot point together at one small, clear, fully tested system diagram.](assets/images/risc-for-ai-software-development/comic-08-reason-together.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *The goal is not minimalism — it is the smallest reliable platform humans and AI can reason about together.*
