## Summary

The article now works as a standalone AI Notes piece for technology leaders. It has clear source handling, broader audience framing, explicit AI-delegation boundaries, role-specific stakes, failure-mode contrasts, a judgment table, a one-week practice loop, and a note that agency, judgment, and persuasion will keep changing in practice as AI develops.

No high-severity editorial or repo-fit issues remain.

## Addressed Findings

- **Nielsen source fidelity** — addressed. The mammoth-hunter line is now framed as an analogy Nielsen uses, not as the exact closing sentence of the article.
- **Examples read as unsourced case studies** — addressed. The post now introduces them as illustrative composites and labels them `Scenario:`.
- **Book/manuscript dependency** — addressed. Explicit book/chapter/manuscript framing has been removed.
- **Over-broad AI capability claims** — mostly addressed. The post now separates current AI assistance from frontier experimentation and uses softer wording such as "may increasingly".
- **Unsourced Hohpe quotation** — addressed. The quote was replaced with a sourced paraphrase and a `To Probe Further` link.
- **Missing spec** — addressed. `spec.md` exists and matches the current article direction.
- **Title casing** — addressed.
- **Empty image alt text** — addressed for all markdown images.
- **Manual `<br>` spacing** — addressed.
- **Alienating phrasing around cautious readers** — addressed. The article now distinguishes blocked experimentation from legitimate governance concerns.
- **Audience too narrow** — addressed. The article and spec now target technology leaders broadly: principal engineers, staff engineers, engineering managers/directors, VPs of Engineering, CTOs, and architects.
- **Examples too architecture-heavy for the broader audience** — addressed. The judgment scenario now focuses on AI-assisted development adoption and rollout.
- **Legacy heading echo** — addressed. The heading is now `Keep Fundamentals Sharp—But Look Up`.
- **Dense opening role list** — addressed. The list has been shortened while keeping the key roles visible.
- **Unclear distinction between final action sections** — addressed. The article now uses `Immediate Practices: What to Do This Week` for near-term habits and `Mindset Shifts for the AI Transition Era` for broader orientation.
- **Need for more depth** — addressed. The opening, judgment section, and immediate practices now carry more concrete detail without expanding the mindset section.
- **Need to show failure modes** — addressed. Each of agency, judgment, and persuasion now includes negative and positive examples.
- **Need to explain why these skills cannot be delegated to AI** — addressed. The article now has a dedicated section on the boundary between AI assistance and human leadership accountability.
- **Need to show that these skills evolve with AI** — addressed. The mindset section now says the capabilities are durable, but their day-to-day practice will change as AI improves.

- The central thesis is now broader and clearer: technology leaders should shift value from artifact production toward agency, judgment, and persuasion.
- The revised post is much better aligned with the intended audience beyond architects.
- Source attribution is materially stronger.
- The scenarios are concrete without pretending to be sourced case studies.
- The scenario set now covers both technical design and operating-model leadership.
- The two concluding guidance sections now have clearer separation: one is practical and immediate; the other is strategic and longer-term.
- The added judgment table sharpens the article's core distinction between generated outputs and leadership accountability.
- The one-week practice loop gives the reader a concrete way to apply the article without launching a formal program.
- The failure-mode contrasts clarify why each skill matters, not only what the skill is.
- The AI-delegation boundary section strengthens the thesis by explaining why leadership remains necessary even when AI can generate useful artifacts.
- The evolving-practice paragraph prevents the skills from reading as static advice.
- The spec is concise and useful; it records the main editorial decisions that shaped the revised post.
- The generated site builds successfully with the spec link.
