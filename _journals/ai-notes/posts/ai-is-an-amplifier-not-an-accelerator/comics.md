![Comic panel: an excited robot stomps a giant gas pedal labeled AI and zooms ahead while an engineer watches skeptically.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-01-the-pedal.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *The comfortable story: AI is a pedal. Press it, go faster.*

![Comic panel: an engineer taps the robot's chest, which is a triangular amplifier dial, while a discarded gas pedal lies on the floor.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-02-not-a-pedal.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *But AI doesn't add speed. It multiplies. It's an amplifier, not a pedal.*

![Comic panel: a small green plus-sign enters the robot's gain dial and comes out as a huge green plus-sign.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-03-good-input-amplified.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *Feed it a strength — a skill, a clean codebase, a clear goal — and it makes it bigger.*

![Comic panel: a small red minus-sign enters the robot's gain dial and comes out as a huge red minus-sign, knocking the robot back.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-04-bad-input-amplified.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *Feed it a mess and the same dial makes that bigger too. The amplifier doesn't care about the sign.*

![Comic panel: a developer runs on a treadmill cheered by the robot, a speedometer reads minus nineteen percent while their thought bubble says it feels twenty percent faster.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-05-felt-fast-was-slow.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *Measured: 19% slower. Felt: 20% faster. The amplifier can run backwards — and you won't feel it.*

![Comic panel: the robot hands over a tiny one-dollar tag for generating code while a huge mountain of review, test, debug and maintain boxes looms with an expensive price tag.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-06-cheap-to-make-costly-to-own.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Cheap to generate, expensive to own. The bill just moved downstream — and got bigger.*

![Comic panel: the robot blasts a firehose of code blocks that pile up and jam at a narrow doorway labeled review and legacy, with a cracked legacy box wobbling.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-07-the-bottleneck.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *Amplify the fast stage and the work just jams the bottleneck behind it — review, integration, fragile legacy. The system runs at the speed of the part you didn't speed up.*

![Comic panel: the robot reaches for a bigger-gain knob but the engineer stops him and polishes the small green plus-sign on the input side instead.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-08-fix-the-input.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *So stop cranking the dial. The leverage was never the multiplier — it's the input, and the part, you choose to amplify.*

![Comic panel: an engineer stands beside the robot whose gain dial glows steady green, under the hand-lettered question 'amplify what?'.](assets/images/ai-is-an-amplifier-not-an-accelerator/comic-09-amplify-what.jpeg)
**Panel 9:** *The amplifier is real, large, and indifferent. The only question that matters is: amplify what?*
