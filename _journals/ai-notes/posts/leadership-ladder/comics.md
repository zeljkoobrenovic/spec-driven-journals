From command to intent: how leaders should work with increasingly capable AI — in eight panels.

![Comic panel: a one-line command gets an instant glossy but generic strategy document from the robot.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-01-command-clerk.jpeg)
**Panel 1:** *Commands work for small tasks — but for leadership work they produce polish without context, judgment, or legitimacy.*

![Comic panel: a generated document with a paper crown sits at the head of the table until the leader removes it.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-02-false-delegation.jpeg)
**Panel 2:** *The biggest risk is false delegation: generated artifacts start being treated as if they contain judgment. They do not.*

![Comic panel: split scene contrasting a flat 'do this' command with a structured intent statement that invites questions.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-03-language-shift.jpeg)
**Panel 3:** *Marquet's lesson, adapted: 'do this' creates a different operating mode than 'I intend X, under constraints Y, stop if Z.'*

![Comic panel: a seven-rung ladder of interaction levels with the robot midway and the leader steadying the base.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-04-the-ladder.jpeg)
**Panel 4:** *The Human-AI Leadership Ladder: seven levels from command to monitored autonomy. Lower rungs are not bad — mismatches are.*

![Comic panel: a robot drives a bulldozer labeled agent mode guided only by a sticky note reading 'do stuff'.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-05-wrong-level.jpeg)
**Panel 5:** *Most leadership failures on the ladder: high-autonomy tools driven with low-maturity language.*

![Comic panel: a bridge made of a spec document connects human intent and AI execution across a gap.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-06-spec-contract.jpeg)
**Panel 6:** *Spec-driven development is intent written down: the inspectable boundary between human purpose and AI execution.*

![Comic panel: the robot high on the ladder wears safety gear labeled scope, logging, review, stop conditions, and rollback.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-07-guardrails-scale.jpeg)
**Panel 7:** *Guardrails scale with autonomy. Autonomy without observability is not delegation — it is drift.*

![Comic panel: leader and robot at a whiteboard under a framed motto reading 'here is my intent' replacing 'do what I say'.](assets/images/leadership-ladder/comic-08-command-to-intent.jpeg)
**Panel 8:** *The real ladder is not from human to AI — it is from command to intent, with accountability staying human.*
