> **Status**: On hold
>
> **Decision**: We will write and maintain MADR's own tooling for ADR creation, rather than rely on existing tools that either declined a MADR contribution or fall out of sync with the format.

## Context

Developers like tooling that scaffolds ADRs quickly. That tooling should support MADR and be easy to install.

Options considered:

- Contribute MADR support to [adr-tools](https://github.com/npryce/adr-tools) (924 stars as of 2018-06-14).
- Contribute MADR support to [adr-j](https://github.com/adoble/adr-j) (2 stars as of 2018-06-14).
- Contribute MADR support to [adr](https://github.com/phodal/adr) (72 stars as of 2018-06-14).
- Write MADR's own tooling.
- No tool support.

## Decision

Write and maintain **MADR's own tooling**.

## Rationale

1. **`adr-tools` declined the contribution.** A [pull request](https://github.com/npryce/adr-tools/pull/43) adding MADR support was rejected, closing the upstream path.
2. **Other tools diverge.** They either modify MADR's structure in ways we don't want, or fall behind MADR releases.
3. **Aligned tooling and template.** Maintaining MADR-native tools keeps tooling and template in lock-step without negotiating each change with a third party.

## Implications

1. **Maintenance cost.** We accept that running our own tooling means we own its bugs and releases.
2. **Faster iteration.** Format changes can ship with the tools that support them.
3. **Visibility.** We will maintain an overview of MADR tooling at <https://adr.github.io/madr/tooling.html>.

## Conclusion

Owning the tooling costs maintenance time but removes the coordination tax of upstream contribution. The *on hold* status reflects that the implementation work is ongoing — the *direction* (rather than relying on `adr-tools`) is settled.
