> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: We will use [MADR](https://adr.github.io/madr/) 4.0.0 as the format for recording architectural and other significant decisions in this project. MADR is lean enough to actually get written and structured enough to stay readable as the log grows.

## Context

We want to record decisions made in this project — architectural, code-level, or otherwise. Doing so requires picking a format and structure that the team will actually use.

Candidates considered:

- **[MADR](https://adr.github.io/madr/) 4.0.0** — Markdown Architectural Decision Records template.
- **[Michael Nygard's template](http://thinkrelevance.com/blog/2011/11/15/documenting-architecture-decisions)** — the original ADR template that coined the term.
- **[Sustainable Architectural Decisions / Y-Statements](https://www.infoq.com/articles/sustainable-architectural-design-decisions)** — a terse one-liner format.
- **Other templates** listed at <https://github.com/joelparkerhenderson/architecture_decision_record>.
- **Formless** — no convention at all.

## Decision

Adopt **MADR 4.0.0** as the template for all decision records.

## Rationale

1. **Implicit assumptions become explicit.** Writing the design rationale down enables future readers to understand the *why*. See ["A rational design process: How and why to fake it"](https://doi.org/10.1109/TSE.1986.6312940).
2. **MADR is general.** It captures any kind of decision, not just architectural ones, with the same structure.
3. **MADR is lean.** The template stays out of the way of a quick decision but scales up when more rigor is needed.
4. **Comprehensible and maintainable.** The structure is easy to read, easy to write, and easy to keep up to date.
5. **Active project.** MADR is alive and responsive to community input.

## Implications

1. **One format to learn.** New contributors learn MADR once rather than negotiating per-decision how to write things down.
2. **Lazy migration.** Existing decisions in other formats can be migrated when they need to be edited, not preemptively.
3. **Tooling investment.** Editors, generators, and TOC tools can be tuned to one format.

## Conclusion

MADR fits our needs: explicit enough to be useful, lean enough to actually get written, and structured enough that the decision log stays usable over time.
