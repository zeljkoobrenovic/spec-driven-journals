> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: MADR adds **`consulted`** and **`informed`** as optional metadata fields, alongside `decision-makers`. The other two RACI roles (Responsible and Accountable) are not adopted.

## Context

We noticed an overlap between MADR's `decision-makers` field and the [RACI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Responsibility_assignment_matrix) responsibility model. Users have asked for fields to record who was consulted and who needs to be informed about a decision. Should MADR adopt parts of RACI to support this?

MADR issue: [#62](https://github.com/adr/madr/issues/62).

## Decision Drivers

- **MADR should contain fields important to the ADR decision process.**
- **The MADR template should be easy to understand.**
- **MADR should stay lightweight.**

## Considered Options

- Include `Consulted` and `Informed` from RACI.
- Include all four RACI fields (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed).
- Include none of RACI.

## Decision

Adopt **`Consulted` and `Informed`** as optional fields.

## Rationale

1. **Well-understood roles.** "Consulted" and "Informed" are widely understood and map cleanly to people-around-the-decision.
2. **Optional keeps MADR lightweight.** Authors who don't need the fields can omit them entirely.
3. **Cost is bounded.** Two additional optional fields, no structural change to the template.

## Implications

1. **Two more optional metadata keys** in the YAML front matter.
2. **Tooling that displays metadata** should render them when present and ignore them when absent.

## Rejected alternative

- **Include all of RACI.** Adding Responsible and Accountable creates confusion: people often disagree on who is "accountable" versus "responsible", and consensus-driven decisions made in small committees often have no single accountable individual. The cost of explaining the model exceeds the benefit of completeness.

## Conclusion

Taking only the two unambiguous roles from RACI gives users the information they asked for without the model's most contentious distinctions.
