> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: The heading for the section recording how a decision was implemented is **`Confirmation`**.

## Context

MADR includes a section where the author describes how the decision will be checked to have been put into practice. The question is what to call that section. The candidates are all near-synonyms in everyday English but carry different technical baggage in software contexts.

## Decision Drivers

- **Consistent with terms used in IT.**
- **Common word.**

## Considered Options

- `Confirmation`
- `Validation`
- `Verification`

## Decision

Use **`Confirmation`**.

## Rationale

1. **`Validation` is out of scope.** In software practice, "validation" refers to the process leading to a *valid* ADR — review, approval, and acceptance — not to confirming the decision was carried out.
2. **`Verification` implies formal procedure.** In IT, "verification" often means a formal tool or procedure (model checking, formal proof). MADR's check is typically less formal than that.
3. **`Confirmation` covers both formal and informal checks.** It does not imply a method, just that *something* was looked at to confirm the decision was implemented.

## Implications

1. **Templates and examples use `Confirmation`** as the section heading.
2. **The section is optional** — not every ADR has something to confirm.

## Confirmation (for this ADR)

- **Before the next release:** check that at least one example includes a `Confirmation` section.
- **Before each release:** check that templates and examples are consistent with this decision (or update the ADR if they intentionally diverge).
- **Continuously:** monitor [GitHub issues](https://github.com/adr/madr/issues) for feedback on examples and tool/template change requests.
