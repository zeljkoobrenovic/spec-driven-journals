> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: MADR is dual-licensed as `MIT OR CC0-1.0`. The recipient may pick whichever license fits their work.

## Context

Without an explicit license, default copyright applies — and in some jurisdictions (e.g. Germany) that means users cannot alter the work without explicit permission. See <https://help.github.com/articles/licensing-a-repository/>. We want users to adopt and adapt MADR with no friction.

Options considered:

- **[CC0](https://creativecommons.org/share-your-work/public-domain/cc0/)** — public-domain dedication.
- **BSD-3** — permissive with attribution.
- **MIT** — permissive, lean.
- **Dual license: MIT OR CC0** — recipient chooses.
- **No license** — copyright default applies.
- **Other open-source licenses** — broadly considered.

## Decision

Adopt **dual licensing under `MIT OR CC0-1.0`**. The SPDX identifier `MIT OR CC0-1.0` is the canonical way to express this and lets the recipient pick whichever license fits.

## Rationale

1. **MIT is documentation-friendly.** MIT is explicitly suitable for documentation and is lean enough to attach without ceremony.
2. **CC0 removes friction.** CC0 hands the work to the public domain as legally as possible — useful for downstream reuse, though it lacks attribution.
3. **Dual licensing offers freedom to the recipient.** Whoever uses MADR picks the license that fits their project, rather than having one imposed.

## Implications

1. **Attribution is not enforced.** Users who pick CC0 are free to redistribute without crediting the project. That is a feature, not a bug, but worth being aware of.
2. **Less well-known model.** Dual licensing is less common than single-license repositories. We rely on the SPDX identifier to make the model machine-readable.
3. **Rejected options.**
   - **CC0 alone** — lacks attribution, which matters for some users ([why attribution is important](https://opensource.stackexchange.com/a/9126/5671)).
   - **BSD-3 alone** — [unclear whether it can be used for documentation](https://opensource.stackexchange.com/a/9545/5671).
   - **MIT alone** — would deny downstream users the CC0 option.
   - **No license** — blocks reuse by default.

## Conclusion

`MIT OR CC0-1.0` gives MADR's users the broadest set of options with the lowest friction. The slightly higher cost of explaining the dual license is small compared to the cost of imposing a single one.
