> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: The `Decision Outcome` section appears **before** the `Pros and Cons of the Options` section.

## Context

A MADR ADR lists the options' pros and cons in detail. The question is where that detailed list belongs in the document — before or after the decision itself.

## Decision Drivers

- **The most important information should be above the fold.**
- **MADR should be easy to write.**
- **MADR should be easy to read.**

## Considered Options

- `Pros and Cons of the Options` after `Decision Outcome`.
- `Pros and Cons of the Options` before `Decision Outcome`.

## Decision

Put `Pros and Cons of the Options` **after** `Decision Outcome`:

```markdown
## Considered Options

...

## Decision Outcome

...

## Pros and Cons of the Options

...
```

## Rationale

1. **Decision is above the fold.** Readers who are skimming get the outcome quickly, without scrolling through detailed analysis.
2. **Options and outcome stay close together.** The reader sees the considered options and the chosen outcome side by side, before drilling into per-option detail.
3. **Pros and Cons reads as an appendix.** "Considered Options" implicitly invites "for a more detailed analysis of these options, refer to Pros and Cons" — and the bottom placement matches that mental model.

## Implications

1. **Slight logical inversion.** Some readers may find it odd that the outcome appears before the arguments that justify it. We accept this in exchange for scannability.
2. **Small MADRs work without the section.** Short decisions with just options and an outcome don't need a Pros and Cons section at all.

## Rejected alternative

- **Pros and Cons before Decision Outcome.** Preserves the "arguments first, conclusion second" logical flow, but pushes the outcome below the fold and makes scanning harder.

## Conclusion

Scannability wins over strict logical flow. Most readers want to know *what was decided* first and *why* second.
