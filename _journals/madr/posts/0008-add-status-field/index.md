> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: An ADR's status is recorded in YAML front matter, not in the body of the document.

## Context

ADRs have a status — proposed, accepted, deprecated, superseded, and so on. We need to decide whether to record it, and if so, how.

Technical story: <https://github.com/adr/madr/issues/2>.

Options considered:

- Use YAML front matter.
- Use a badge image.
- Use a plain text line (e.g. `Status: Accepted`).
- Use a separate Markdown heading.
- Use a table.
- Do not record status at all.

## Decision

Use **YAML front matter** for the status field. Example:

```markdown
---
parent: Decisions
nav_order: 3
status: on hold
---
# Write own MADR tooling
```

## Rationale

1. **Widely supported.** YAML front matter is parsed by most Markdown tooling, including Jekyll, Hugo, and the major static-site generators.
2. **Out of the body.** The opening of the ADR stays focused on context and problem statement, rather than leading with bureaucracy.
3. **Machine-readable.** Tools can filter, sort, and badge ADRs by status without parsing prose.

## Implications

1. **Renderer support required.** Renderers must strip front matter from the body. Most modern Markdown tooling does this; a few do not.
2. **Authors learn one convention.** A consistent YAML block at the top of every ADR.
3. **Rejected alternatives.**
   - **Badge images** — depend on `shields.io` or local generation; many badges to maintain; hard to read in source.
   - **Plain text line** — works, but pushes the actual content of the ADR below the fold.
   - **Separate heading** — uses three lines for one piece of metadata.
   - **Table** — not in the CommonMark spec; harder to read; history hard to identify.
   - **No status** — users have demanded the field and other ADR templates include it.
