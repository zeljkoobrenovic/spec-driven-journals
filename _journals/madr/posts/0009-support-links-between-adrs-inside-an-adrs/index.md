> **Status**: Accepted
>
> **Decision**: Links from one ADR to another are placed in a `## More Information` section at the bottom of the document.

## Context

A decision often references other decisions — supersedes, follows up on, is informed by. MADR should support these cross-links.

Technical story: <https://github.com/adr/madr/issues/9>.

Options considered:

- Include links in a `More Information` section.
- Use a table of related decisions.
- A heading + bullet list immediately after the status.
- A heading + bullet list immediately after `Decision Outcome`.
- A heading + bullet list at the end of the document.
- Do not support links.

## Decision

Place cross-ADR links in a `## More Information` section. Example:

```markdown
## More Information

[ADR-0008](0008-add-status-field.md) reasons on adding meta data (such as status).
```

## Rationale

1. **Freedom for the author.** The section is unstructured prose, so authors can write links in whatever shape fits the relationship (supersedes, related to, informed by, etc.).
2. **Bottom placement matches reading order.** The links are useful *after* the reader understands the decision, not before.

## Implications

1. **Parsing is harder.** Tooling that wants to extract relationships must parse prose. We accept that cost.
2. **Convention, not schema.** Authors should follow conventions (e.g. "Supersedes ADR-0001") consistently to make grep useful.

## Rejected alternatives

- **Tables** — not in CommonMark, hard to read raw, and don't show clearly when a link has been superseded.
- **Heading + bullet list after status** — inconsistent with status as a one-liner; clutters the opening.
- **Heading + bullet list after `Decision Outcome`** — readers expect explanation of the options at that point, not unrelated cross-references.
- **Heading + bullet list at the very end without the `More Information` framing** — works, but `More Information` is a clearer label.
- **No links** — keeps the template minimal but ignores a real authoring need.

## Conclusion

A free-form `## More Information` section is the cheapest way to support cross-ADR links without imposing a brittle schema.
