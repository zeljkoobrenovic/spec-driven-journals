> **Status**: DRAFT
>
> **Decision**: Organization will define staff-plus engineering as a real technical leadership path. Staff, principal, and equivalent engineers create impact through cross-team technical direction, architecture advice, mentoring, standards, complex problem solving, and capability building. They influence decisions without becoming hidden managers or approval gates.

## Decision

Adopt an explicit **staff-plus technical leadership model**.

Staff-plus engineers should:

* operate at a scope larger than a single task or feature;
* improve the technical direction and execution quality of multiple engineers or teams;
* help teams make better decisions through advice, examples, and ADRs;
* mentor engineers and tech leads;
* identify and resolve cross-team technical risks;
* turn repeated problems into standards, platform capabilities, or learning material;
* partner with engineering managers, product leaders, architects, and platform teams.

The role is not a reward for being "very senior" in place. It is a change in leverage.

## How to Read This Decision

| What it says | What it does **not** say |
| --- | --- |
| Staff-plus is a leadership path. | Staff-plus engineers manage people by default. |
| Influence should be visible and recorded. | Staff-plus engineers need formal veto power. |
| Cross-team scope matters. | A staff engineer cannot write production code. |
| Mentoring and standards are part of impact. | Staff-plus work is only meetings and documents. |
| Staff-plus roles need clear missions. | Every senior engineer should become staff. |

Staff-plus leadership succeeds when the organisation becomes more technically capable even when the staff-plus engineer is not in the room.

## How to Use This Decision

Use this decision to:

* explain how staff-plus technical leadership differs from senior individual contribution;
* recognise different staff-plus archetypes and when each is useful;
* identify durable outputs that show technical leadership impact;
* partner staff-plus engineers with managers, product, architecture, and platform teams without role confusion;
* avoid turning staff-plus engineers into hidden managers or approval gates.

The main lesson is that staff-plus work is not "harder tickets". It is leverage: improving technical direction, decision quality, and capability across a wider scope.

## One-Minute Mental Model

Staff-plus engineers **scale technical judgement through others**.

They still may write code, debug production issues, and design systems. But their distinctive contribution is that other people and teams make better technical decisions because of their work. That can happen through mentoring, ADRs, reference designs, migration strategies, standards, incident learning, or cross-team initiative leadership.

If impact disappears when the staff-plus engineer leaves the meeting, the work has not scaled yet.

## Rationale

As organisations grow, not all important technical work fits inside one team:

* architectural consistency across product streams;
* platform adoption and exception handling;
* migrations and legacy retirement;
* reliability and performance improvements;
* mentoring new tech leads;
* reducing duplicated patterns;
* helping teams navigate ambiguous tradeoffs.

If this work is left informal, it either disappears or becomes hidden power. If it is pushed into management, technical experts must change career track to have influence. A staff-plus model gives technical leadership a clear shape and accountability.

## Staff-Plus Archetypes

Different staff-plus engineers may create leverage in different ways:

| Archetype | Primary contribution |
| --- | --- |
| Tech lead at scale | Leads a large initiative across several teams while preserving team ownership. |
| Architect / systems thinker | Shapes architecture, boundaries, standards, and long-term technical direction. |
| Solver / firefighter | Handles the hardest incidents or technical knots, then removes recurrence. |
| Practice builder | Raises engineering practice through coaching, tooling, templates, and communities. |
| Domain expert | Carries deep knowledge in a critical domain or subsystem and transfers it responsibly. |

The archetype should be explicit enough that expectations are clear. Ambiguous staff-plus roles become status labels.

## Operating Expectations

Staff-plus work should produce durable evidence:

| Work | Durable output |
| --- | --- |
| Architecture advice | ADR comments, AAF advice, reference architecture, decision notes. |
| Cross-team initiative | Written problem statement, options, decision records, rollout plan, revisit criteria. |
| Mentoring | Tech lead growth, documented patterns, improved review quality. |
| Incident or reliability work | Post-incident learning, SLO changes, platform or design improvements. |
| Standard setting | Principle, guideline, template, golden path, or governance-as-code rule. |
| Legacy simplification | Ownership map, migration plan, retirement evidence. |

The output does not need to be heavy. It needs to outlast private conversations.

## Partnership Boundaries

| Partner | Healthy relationship |
| --- | --- |
| Engineering manager | Staff-plus shapes technical direction and capability; EM shapes people system, staffing, and performance. |
| Product partner | Staff-plus explains feasibility, tradeoffs, and technical opportunities; product clarifies value and priority. |
| Architect | Staff-plus contributes real system context; architect helps align cross-domain constraints. |
| Platform team | Staff-plus helps turn repeated team pain into platform capabilities. |
| Team tech lead | Staff-plus mentors and advises without taking local ownership away. |

The recurring discipline is to strengthen accountable teams, not route work around them.

## Worked Example

A principal engineer sees three teams independently creating incompatible event patterns.

Low-leverage response:

* Review every design personally.
* Tell teams which pattern to use.
* Become the person everyone asks before changing event code.

Staff-plus response:

| Action | Leverage created |
| --- | --- |
| Map the patterns and consequences. | Makes the problem visible without blaming teams. |
| Facilitate advice across affected teams. | Brings expertise together early. |
| Draft an ADR or reference pattern. | Creates a durable decision artifact. |
| Pair with platform on reusable support. | Turns repeated advice into capability. |
| Mentor team tech leads through adoption. | Grows local ownership instead of central dependency. |

The principal engineer may still do deep technical work. The important part is that the organisation becomes less dependent on private judgement.

## Anti-Patterns

* **Shadow manager.** Staff-plus engineer assigns work, handles performance signals, or becomes the real manager without accountability.
* **Approval gate.** Teams believe staff-plus sign-off is required even when no decision says so.
* **Hero specialist.** The staff-plus engineer solves problems personally but leaves no capability behind.
* **Status without mission.** The title exists, but scope, expectations, and outcomes are unclear.
* **Architecture by opinion.** Advice is given as preference without evidence, constraints, or tradeoffs.
* **Manager substitute.** A senior IC is asked to compensate for missing management, product, or platform ownership.

## Discussion Prompts

Use these prompts when defining a staff-plus role or mission:

* What scope is larger than one team, and why does it need senior technical leadership?
* Which decisions, systems, or practices should improve because this role exists?
* What durable outputs will remain after the work?
* Which teams should become more capable through this person's influence?
* Where could this role accidentally become an approval gate?
* Which manager, product, platform, or architecture partners must stay aligned?

## Scope and Revisiting

This ADR applies to staff, principal, distinguished, architect, and equivalent senior technical IC roles.

Revisit if staff-plus roles become ambiguous status levels, hidden approval gates, or substitutes for missing management and platform ownership.
