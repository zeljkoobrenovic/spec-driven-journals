# Spec: Team Size and Composition Guardrails

> Filled in during the second pass over backfilled specs. Status `draft`.

## Intent

State that Organization uses **explicit guardrails for team size and
composition**: a healthy product engineering team has 5-8 engineers,
one clear product partner, design access where UX matters, technical
leadership, and enough delivery/quality/data/operational skill to own
its stream. Teams below 4 engineers are **fragile**; teams above 10
core contributors should usually **split or narrow scope**.

## Audience

- **Engineering managers** sizing teams.
- **Senior leadership** approving org changes.
- **Product / engineering partnership** evaluating team formation.
- **HR / org design** modelling team structures.

## Success criteria

- [ ] Reader can state the **5-8 engineer** sweet spot.
- [ ] Reader knows below 4 is **fragile**, above 10 needs splitting.
- [ ] Reader can name the cross-functional roles needed.

## Non-goals

- Defining specific team boundaries.
- Replacing the org chart.

## Open questions

- How to handle genuinely large problems that don't naturally split.

## Decision log

- **2026-05-20** — Set **explicit numeric guardrails** (5-8, <4,
  >10). Considered "small enough, no number"; rejected because
  numbers force the conversation when teams drift.
