# Spec: Roadmap — Six Phases from Visibility to Legacy Retirement

> Filled in during the second pass over backfilled specs. Status `draft`;
> keep this spec current with substantive post changes.

## Intent

Translate diagnosis, policy, and operating model into a **practical
six-phase sequence**: Phase 0 control & visibility, Phase 1 delivery
path, Phase 2 ownership clarity, Phase 3 backend convergence, Phase 4
infrastructure simplification, Phase 5 priority legacy retirement. The
roadmap is a **frame**, not a project plan; exact dates and named
projects come from [[prioritization]] once first targets are validated.

## Audience

- **Engineering leadership** seeing the multi-phase arc.
- **[[operate]] forum** scoping work into the right phase.
- **Strategy owner** monitoring phase transitions.
- **Domain owners** of the phase deliverables.

## Success criteria

- [ ] Reader can name the six phases in order.
- [ ] Reader knows phases sequence by **highest-friction-first**, not
      easiest-first.
- [ ] Reader understands the roadmap is a frame, not a Gantt chart.
- [ ] Reader sees how the roadmap connects to [[first-targets]] and
      [[prioritization]].

## Non-goals

- Exact dates, named projects, or team-by-team sequencing.
- Headcount or budget allocation.
- Pre-committing to phase-end success criteria (those come per
  wave).

## Open questions

- Concrete signals for phase transition (when does Phase 1 end and
  Phase 2 begin in practice).
- Whether Phase 0 deserves to be a phase at all or should be
  treated as continuous baseline work.

## Decision log

- **2026-05-20** — Chose **highest-friction-first** sequencing.
  Considered easiest-first; rejected because easy-first roadmaps
  produce visible work but don't move the strategy.
- **2026-05-20** — Made the roadmap **a frame, not a plan**.
  Considered specifying dates; rejected because dates without
  validated first targets produce false confidence.
- **2026-05-20** — Included **Phase 0** (control & visibility)
  rather than starting at Phase 1. Considered skipping it; rejected
  because without baseline visibility, later phases have no
  measurable starting point.
