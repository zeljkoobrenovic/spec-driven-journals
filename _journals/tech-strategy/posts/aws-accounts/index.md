> **Status**: DRAFT
>
> **Decision**: Use AVIV's central AWS Foundation and Security guidance as the starting point for AWS account usage in Organization Germany. Keep networking centrally managed in the initial phase. Create accounts only where ownership, isolation, or compliance clearly requires them, and only with explicit owner, purpose, and lifecycle status. Consolidate first on accounts with unclear ownership or purpose, transitional accounts that have become permanent, and duplicated environment structures with limited strategic value. No new Germany-specific foundation divergence in the initial phase. The current estate of roughly 200 accounts is the starting point, not the steady state.

## Decision

This document is the **AWS account blueprint** (the default principles for account usage) and the **consolidation plan** (how the current estate moves toward a governable structure).

The account estate is one of the **clearest visible signs of structural fragmentation** in [[diagnosis]]: roughly 200 accounts, split between a legacy account estate and a White-Label-era account estate, with a long tail of transitional or unclear-purpose accounts. Many were created when each team had its own workspace with three AWS accounts (`dev`, `preview`, `live`) - an account-as-workspace model that, as team names and responsibilities changed, became an account-as-archaeology problem.

## Why This Matters

The cloud estate split is structurally **two estates, not one**:

* About **113 legacy AWS accounts**.
* Roughly **100 White-Label-era accounts**.
* A long tail of **transitional or unclear-purpose accounts**.

This sizing was reasonable for a multi-brand, multi-country White-Label platform. It is **not the right shape** for a stable single-brand business with Germany's current scope (implication 2 in [[implications]]).

The job of this blueprint is to make sure the estate **stops growing in the wrong direction**, and the consolidation plan is the path to bring the existing estate closer to Germany's actual operating scope.

## How to Use This Blueprint

Use this blueprint when creating, reviewing, consolidating, or retiring AWS accounts. It should answer:

* Why does this workload or estate need a separate account boundary?
* Who owns the account operationally and who can decide its future?
* Is the account active, transitional, legacy, or unclear?
* Which central AVIV Foundation or Security pattern applies?
* Does this account reduce risk and clarity, or preserve account sprawl?
* What review date or retirement path exists if the account is transitional?

The blueprint is deliberately conservative: stop new account sprawl first, then consolidate accounts whose purpose, ownership, or lifecycle no longer matches Germany's operating shape.

## What This Should Help You Do

This blueprint should help a reader:

* Explain why the current account estate is a fragmentation signal.
* Decide when a separate AWS account boundary is justified.
* Classify accounts by owner, purpose, lifecycle status, and review point.
* Identify first consolidation candidates without inventing a new Germany-specific foundation.

Common misreadings to avoid:

| Misreading | Correction |
| --- | --- |
| "Fewer accounts is always better." | The goal is governable account boundaries, not a simplistic count target. |
| "Central AVIV guidance removes local ownership." | Central guidance is the starting pattern; Germany still needs explicit account ownership. |
| "Transitional accounts are harmless." | Transitional accounts become legacy unless reviewed and retired. |

## Default Account Shape

### Starting Point

* **Start from central AVIV AWS Foundation and Security guidance.**
* **Keep networking centrally managed** in the initial phase.
* **Create accounts only where ownership, isolation, or compliance clearly requires them.**
* **Align account boundaries with real operational responsibility.**

### Required Characteristics

Every account should have:

* **Named owner.**
* **Explicit purpose.**
* **Lifecycle status**: active, transitional, or legacy.
* **Mapped relationship** to a system, team, or platform.
* **Review point** if transitional or legacy.

An account that does not meet these requirements is **not strategy-compliant**. It is legacy for the purposes of [[policy]] P4, and needs a declared status.

### Transitional Direction

While the estate is moving toward the default:

* **No new accounts** without explicit owner and purpose.
* **No permanent transitional accounts.** Transitional means temporary.
* **No local Germany-specific foundation divergence** in the initial phase.
* **Review existing accounts** for consolidation, containment, or retirement.

The first three rules are how the estate stops growing in the wrong direction. The fourth is how it starts shrinking.

## Allowed and Not Allowed

### Allowed

* **Use of centrally defined account and networking patterns.**
* **Temporary transitional accounts** during migration (with review point).
* **Explicit exceptions** where central patterns do not fit (through [[operate]]).
* **Keeping legacy accounts temporarily** when the status is explicit and reviewed.

### Not Allowed by Default

* **Creating accounts by local preference alone.** A team wanting their own account is not a reason.
* **Unowned or purpose-unclear accounts.** "Someone created this in 2021" is not a purpose.
* **Germany-specific foundation divergence** in the initial phase.
* **Permanent transitional accounts** with no review point.

The "by default" matters. Each of these can be approved through the exception process; they just cannot happen invisibly.

## Decision Questions

When proposing or reviewing an account:

* **Why does this need a separate account?**
* **Who owns it?**
* **What would break if it shared an existing approved pattern?**
* **Is this reducing long-term fragmentation, or increasing it?**

The fourth question is the most often skipped. Every new account is a small bet against the rest of the consolidation plan; making the bet explicit is part of the policy.

## Consolidation Plan

The blueprint above is the **default shape**. The consolidation plan below is how the current estate moves toward it.

### Current Situation

The known AWS estate includes:

| Group | Approximate count | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Legacy AWS estate (`iwnue-*`, `iwhh-*`, older `iw-*`) | ~113 | Monolith, search, payment, Kafka, API gateway, DNS, other legacy services. Fragmented ownership. |
| White-Label-era estate | ~100 | WL seeker/owner and intermediary domains. Often more aligned with central foundation. |
| Transitional / unclear-purpose accounts | Unknown long tail | Explicit target group: no clear owner, no clear purpose, no review point. |

The current landscape is a **visible sign of structural fragmentation** more than a sign of any individual mistake.

### Convergence Goal

Move toward:

* **Explicit owner and purpose** for every account.
* **Explicit status** for every account: active, transitional, or legacy.
* **Fewer unclear or duplicated** account structures.
* **Tighter control** over new account creation.

### Phased Approach

#### Phase 1. Visibility

* **Classify all known accounts** by owner, purpose, and status.
* **Identify legacy accounts** still active only because no retirement decision was made.
* **Identify transitional accounts** with no review point.
* **Identify duplicated environment structures** with weak strategic value.

The output of Phase 1 is a maintained inventory that the [[operate]] review forum can act on.

#### Phase 2. Consolidation

* **Consolidate or retire** low-value transitional accounts.
* **Tighten account creation rules** through the exception process.
* **Align account boundaries** more closely with real operational ownership (output of [[ownership-clarification]]).

#### Phase 3. Revisit

* **Revisit whether the resulting estate** still needs the current level of account fragmentation.
* **Assess whether central networking and foundation choices** still fit after the first consolidation wave.

This is the phase where the strategy may legitimately diverge from the AVIV Group default, if the evidence supports it. Before that, the burden of proof for divergence is high.

### Immediate Rule

While the consolidation plan is being executed, the **immediate rule** that needs to be enforced from day one:

> **Do not create new AWS accounts without:**
>
> * explicit **owner**,
> * explicit **purpose**,
> * explicit **lifecycle status**,
> * clear reason why an **existing approved pattern is insufficient**.

This rule applies regardless of whether the consolidation plan has reached the team's area yet. It is the bare minimum for [[policy]] P8.

### Working Assumptions

* The current estate of roughly **200 accounts** is too fragmented for Germany's current business scope.
* **Not all fragmentation can be removed quickly.** Some accounts have legitimate isolation, compliance, or operational reasons.
* **Owner and status clarity must come before aggressive consolidation.** Consolidating accounts that do not have clear owners is how the current estate was created in the first place.

### First Wave Actions

The concrete first-wave work for consolidation:

1. **Produce a complete owner and status map** for the known estate.
2. **Mark every account** as active / transitional / legacy / unknown.
3. **Flag accounts** with:
   * No owner.
   * No purpose.
   * No review point.
   * Duplicated environment structure.
4. **Build the first candidate list** for consolidate / retire / investigate.

The output of these four steps is the first version of the AWS consolidation wave plan that [[operate]] reviews on the monthly cadence.

### Minimum Decision Rules

| Rule | Why |
| --- | --- |
| No new account by default. | The estate is the wrong size already; default no. |
| No unowned account. | Defeats P5 of [[policy]] otherwise. |
| No transitional account without review point. | "Transitional" without a review point is permanent in disguise. |
| No local Germany-specific foundation divergence in the initial phase. | Preserves the option to align with the AVIV Group default until evidence supports otherwise. |

### Success Criteria

The consolidation plan is working when:

* **Every account has an owner and purpose.**
* **Unclear-purpose accounts** are shrinking.
* **Transitional accounts** are not becoming permanent by inertia.
* The account estate becomes **easier to understand and govern**.

These are restatements of [[policy]] P9 in account-specific form.

## First Consolidation Priority

Within the current estate, prioritise review of:

* **Accounts with unclear ownership.**
* **Accounts with unclear purpose.**
* **Duplicated environment structures** with little strategic value.
* **Legacy accounts still active** only because no retirement decision was made.

These are the four categories where the gap between current state and required state is largest, and where action is most realistic.

## What This Blueprint Deliberately Does Not Specify

| Not specified | Why |
| --- | --- |
| Whether networking should ever move out of central management | Initial phase only; revisit in Phase 3. |
| A target number of accounts | A number is the wrong target. The right target is "every account justified". |
| A specific organisational unit (OU) structure | OU structure follows ownership, which is what [[ownership-clarification]] is producing. |
| Whether to introduce Control Tower / Landing Zone Accelerator / etc. | Tooling decisions are made through [[operate]] once the inventory is in place. |
| Whether to require a specific tagging schema | Tagging is part of the operational layer; the blueprint sets the principle, not the schema. |

## Anti-Patterns

* **"Every team should have its own account."** That is exactly the account-as-workspace pattern that produced the current estate.
* **"We can sort out the owner later."** Accounts without owners do not get cleaned up; they get older.
* **"This account is transitional."** Without a review point, "transitional" is a euphemism for "unowned".
* **"We need a Germany-specific landing zone."** Initial-phase divergence from central foundation is exactly the not-allowed-by-default case.
* **"Consolidate first, name owners later."** Consolidation without owners is how the current estate was produced in the first place.

## Scope and Revisiting

Revisit the blueprint when:

* Phase 1 visibility work shows that the estate is materially different from the current diagnosis.
* AVIV Group's central AWS Foundation changes materially.
* Phase 3 evidence supports a Germany-specific divergence from the central foundation.
* The first consolidation wave produces clear lessons about what consolidation patterns work.

In the absence of those triggers, the blueprint should be **stable**.
