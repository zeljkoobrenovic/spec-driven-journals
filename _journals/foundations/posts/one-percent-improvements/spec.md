# Spec: One Percent Improvements as a Weekly Engineering Habit

> Working contract for a foundation record that turns minor weekly
> improvements into a visible engineering habit. The post should make the
> practice small enough to adopt, concrete enough to repeat, and serious
> enough to compound.

## Intent

Propose a lightweight weekly process where each team makes one small
engineering improvement, records it briefly, and shares it with everyone.
The post should connect the practice to two outcomes: visible continuous
progress that improves morale, and meaningful long-term impact from the
cumulative effect of many minor improvements.

## Audience

- **Engineering teams** looking for a repeatable way to improve their local
  environment without waiting for a programme or reorganisation.
- **Engineering managers and tech leads** who want a low-overhead habit for
  morale, ownership, and operational improvement.
- **Guilds and curators** who can spot repeated improvements, connect similar
  work, and promote patterns into durable records.
- **AI agents** helping teams turn weekly improvement notes into follow-up
  posts, standards, or backlog items.

## Success criteria

- [ ] Reader understands that "1%" is a direction and habit, not a precise
      measurement target.
- [ ] Reader can run the weekly process without a new tool, committee, or
      heavy reporting template.
- [ ] Reader can distinguish minor improvements from large projects and from
      cosmetic activity.
- [ ] Reader sees the two explicit goals: morale through visible progress and
      impact through cumulative improvement.
- [ ] Reader understands the compounding argument without treating it as a
      precise mathematical promise.
- [ ] Reader understands the relationship to technical debt: some one percent
      improvements remove debt, but the habit also catches friction before it
      is formally labelled as debt.
- [ ] Reader has concrete examples of suitable weekly improvements.
- [ ] Reader knows how to share improvements so other teams can copy,
      adapt, or connect them.
- [ ] Reader understands when repeated local improvements should become a
      principle, standard, foundation post, or Architecture Advisory Forum
      topic.
- [ ] Reader will not treat the habit as a productivity quota, surveillance
      mechanism, or substitute for fixing systemic problems.

## Non-goals

- Creating a new performance-management metric.
- Replacing formal technical-debt tracking, prioritisation, or remediation for
  large systemic problems.
- Replacing roadmap planning, incident follow-up, platform investment, or
  architectural decision-making.
- Claiming that every week must produce exactly one percent numerical gain.
- Defining a company-wide improvement backlog or central approval process.
- Turning all improvement notes into durable journal records.

## Open questions

- Which shared channel or forum should become the default place for weekly
  improvement notes?
- Should teams keep a lightweight local log, or is the shared weekly note
  enough at the start?
- How should improvements that expose cross-team blockers be escalated
  without turning the ritual into a status meeting?

## Decision log

- **2026-05-22** — Framed the record as a weekly habit rather than a
  measurement programme because the intended value is sustained action and
  shared learning, not a numeric target.
- **2026-05-22** — Chose "One Percent Improvements" over "Theory of Minor
  Improvements" as the post title because the 1% phrase gives teams a simple
  memory hook while the body can explain the broader theory.
- **2026-05-22** — Kept the sharing mechanism lightweight. Considered a formal
  central registry, but rejected it for the first version because it would
  make small improvements feel like reporting overhead.
- **2026-05-22** — Positioned guilds and curators as pattern spotters, not
  approvers, so the ritual stays owned by teams while still feeding the wider
  engineering corpus.
- **2026-05-22** — Clarified the relationship to technical debt. Chose a
  complementary framing because the weekly habit should remove small debt and
  prevent future debt without becoming a formal debt register.
