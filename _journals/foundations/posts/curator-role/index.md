> **Status**: DRAFT
>
> **Decision**: Organization will establish a Curator role for the engineering record corpus. Curators have editorial authority over record shape, metadata, cross-links, lifecycle, and prose clarity. They do not own the decisions. When an edit would change meaning, the change goes back to the decision owner and, when cross-team impact exists, through the Architecture Advisory Forum.

## Decision

Create a small **Curator** function for records, principles, strategies, and foundations.

| Area | Curator authority |
| --- | --- |
| Structure | Normalize section order, headings, and appendices. |
| Metadata | Keep `id`, `status`, `permalink`, `tags`, and excerpts coherent. |
| Links | Add, repair, and improve cross-record links. |
| Lifecycle | Mark records as proposed, accepted, superseded, deprecated, or archived according to evidence. |
| Prose | Rewrite for clarity without changing meaning. |
| Corpus shape | Recommend splits, merges, or new records when the corpus becomes hard to navigate. |

Curators steward the corpus. They do not become the owners of every decision in it.

## Why This Role Exists

As the record set grows, unmanaged entropy appears:

* similar decisions use different language;
* old records keep looking current;
* cross-links are missing or stale;
* long posts hide their actual decision;
* metadata becomes inconsistent;
* AI agents receive conflicting context.

Curators prevent this without centralizing decision authority.

## Boundary

| Curators may do | Curators may not do alone |
| --- | --- |
| Improve headings, order, summaries, and excerpts. | Change the substance of a decision. |
| Move glossary material to appendices. | Accept, reject, or override a team-owned decision. |
| Add missing related-record links. | Invent a new standard without advice. |
| Fix stale status labels when evidence is clear. | Deprecate a living standard without owner review. |
| Propose splits or merges. | Hide disagreement or remove important trade-offs. |

The boundary test is simple: **would the accountable owner say the decision now means something different?** If yes, it is not a curation-only change.

## Workflow

1. Identify a curation need: unclear structure, stale status, duplicate record, missing links, or confusing prose.
2. Check the record's owner, status, related records, and spec.
3. Make editorial changes through the normal AI-mediated workflow.
4. Preserve `id` and `permalink` unless an explicit migration is approved.
5. Build and review generated pages.
6. Ask the owner or Architecture Advisory Forum for advice when meaning may change.

Curators should leave enough trace in the spec or changelog for future readers to understand why a record moved.

## Quality Bar

A curated record should:

* state its decision or principle early;
* separate decision content from reference material;
* keep vocabulary and templates in appendices;
* link related records instead of repeating them;
* expose consequences and anti-patterns;
* have stable metadata;
* have generated docs that build cleanly.

## What This Means for Teams

* Teams still own their decisions.
* Teams should expect curators to improve structure and clarity.
* Teams should respond when curators flag ambiguity, stale status, or conflicting guidance.
* Teams should use `[[slug]]` links and stable metadata so curation remains easy.
* Teams should not treat prose polish as an ownership challenge.

## Examples

| Situation | Curator action |
| --- | --- |
| A post starts with a long vocabulary section. | Move vocabulary to an appendix and keep the decision path clear. |
| Two records say similar things with different terms. | Add cross-links and propose consolidation if needed. |
| A proposed decision has clearly been adopted in practice. | Ask the owner whether status should move to accepted. |
| A rewrite would change the technical recommendation. | Stop and route the change to the owner or AAF. |

## Anti-Patterns

* **Shadow architecture board.** Curators use editorial authority to override teams.
* **Cosmetic-only curation.** Prose gets prettier while stale decisions remain stale.
* **Metadata churn.** IDs, permalinks, and statuses change without migration thinking.
* **Silent meaning change.** A curator rewrites a decision so it reads as a different choice.
* **Corpus neglect.** Everyone can publish, but nobody maintains the body of records.

## Scope and Revisiting

This role applies to durable engineering records in this repository. It does not apply to private people material, active incident command, or team-local runbooks that are not part of the durable corpus.

Revisit the role if curation becomes a bottleneck, if curators start acting as approvers, or if the corpus grows enough to need explicit journal ownership.
